I'm a product designer who thrives on turning complexity into clarity. Grounded in both user needs and business goals, I approach design as a balancing act—crafting solutions that feel intuitive to the people using them while driving measurable impact for the organizations building them. My experience spans enterprise and consumer-facing applications, primarily within healthcare, where I've learned that good design isn't just about aesthetics, it's about navigating high-stakes, often complicated systems with empathy and intention. I'm currently deepening this practice through a Master's in Human-Computer Interaction, sharpening the research and theory behind the intuition I bring to my work. Outside of design, I'm usually chasing a personal best on a run, working through a crossword, or piecing together a puzzle—habits that, much like design, reward patience, pattern recognition, and a love of figuring things out.

How I work

My Approach

I believe great design is a process, not a moment, built through iteration, informed by research, and shaped by the people around me.

Research-Driven

I let user insights and data guide decisions, not assumptions.

Iterative by Design

I test, learn, and refine continuously, rather than aiming for a perfect first draft.

Balanced by Business & User Needs

I design solutions that work for the people using them and the business behind them.

Collaborative at Heart

I partner closely with cross-functional teams, knowing the best ideas rarely come from working alone.

Holistic in Scope

I zoom out to understand the full context of a project before zooming in on solutions.
